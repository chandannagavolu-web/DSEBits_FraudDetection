# PaySim — KS Statistic & Gini Coefficient Analysis (test split)

Generated by `src/evaluation/evaluate_paysim_ks_gini.py` using the credit-scorecard
utilities in `src/evaluation/ks_gini.py`. Two KS flavours are reported:

- **KS (decile)** — credit-scorecard KS: rank cases into 10 equal-frequency risk
  bands, take the max gap between cumulative %bad and %good. This is the number
  risk teams quote against the industry rule of thumb (**KS > 0.40 = strong**).
- **KS (continuous)** — max gap of the ROC tpr/fpr curves over all thresholds.
- **Gini** = `2*AUC - 1`.

## Key findings

1. **Decile KS saturates at ~0.901 under extreme imbalance.** Fraud prevalence is
   ~0.13%, so the top decile (10% of cases ≈ 14,316 rows) trivially contains every
   fraud. Any model that ranks all 185 test frauds into band 1 therefore reads
   `cum_bad=1.0`, `cum_good≈0.099`, KS ≈ 0.901 — a structural ceiling, not a sign
   of equal model quality. The decile KS cannot separate RF / XGBoost / LogReg here.

2. **Gini and continuous KS DO discriminate** and agree with the PR-AUC / MCC
   ranking: full RF/XGBoost are near-perfect (leakage-inflated), and within the
   honest no-leakage set XGBoost leads (Gini 0.9995, KS_cont 0.991).

3. **The GNN is the only model below the decile-KS ceiling (0.793).** Its KS table
   shows band 1 captures just 165/185 frauds (89.2%); ~20 frauds leak down into
   bands 2–9. So unlike the tabular models it fails to rank every fraud into the
   top decile — a concrete, interpretable expression of the weaker fraud ranking
   already seen in its PR-AUC (0.54) and MCC (0.49).

4. **All models clear the KS > 0.40 "strong model" rule of thumb** — but that rule
   is calibrated for roughly balanced credit populations. At 0.13% prevalence the
   decile KS is inflated, so it should NOT be the deciding metric for this fraud
   dataset.

## Recommendation

- For the **fraud track (PaySim)**, keep **PR-AUC** and **MCC** as the decisive
  metrics; report **Gini** and **continuous KS** as supporting discrimination
  measures. Treat the decile KS as illustrative only, and explicitly note its
  saturation under extreme class imbalance.
- The decile-KS / Gini scorecard view is most meaningful on the **credit-risk
  track** (German Credit, Lending Club), where class balance is far less extreme;
  it is computed here for cross-track metric consistency.
